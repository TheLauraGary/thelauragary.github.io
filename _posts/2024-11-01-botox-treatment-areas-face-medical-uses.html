When most people think of Botox, they think of forehead lines or crow's feet—but Botox is far more versatile.

Common Cosmetic Botox Treatment Areas:

  • Forehead Lines: Smooth horizontal lines that form from raising your eyebrows.
  • Crow's Feet: Softens the fine lines around the outer corners of your eyes.
  • Glabellar Lines ("11s"): Reduces the vertical "11" lines between your eyebrows.
  • Bunny Lines: Minimizes the wrinkles that appear on the bridge of your nose when you smile.
  • Marionette Lines: Softens the lines that run from the corners of your mouth down to your chin.
  • Lip Flip: Gently roll the upper lip outward for a fuller appearance.
  • Chin Dimpling: Relax the chin for a smoother contour.
  • Neck Bands (Platysma): Smooths out horizontal lines and bands on the neck.

Medical Botox Uses:

  • Hyperhidrosis (Excessive Sweating): Botox can be injected into the underarms, hands, or feet to control overactive sweat glands.
  • Chronic Migraines: Injected around the head and neck, Botox helps reduce headache frequency.
  • TMJ and Jaw Tension: Botox can relax the jaw muscles to alleviate teeth grinding and jaw pain.
